# Founder Profile

## Sourced Identity

Theo / T Samuels is building Howling Mune around healing, self-relationship, group dynamics, and a longer-range collective/retreat model. In the April 21 vision call, he described his work as coaching/counseling through self-relationship and group dynamics, with three layers: physical, metaphysical, and ego. In the May 6 discussion, he clarified that he no longer sees the work only as him being an individual healer who can serve a limited number of people each week; he is moving toward a scalable collective model.

## Sourced Authority

- He has existing client demand: the April 21 transcript includes a moment where a client asks him for a call during the conversation.
- He has real background in photography, advertising/marketing education, anthropology/religion curiosity, coaching certifications, and a long self-education path.
- He has done or envisioned group/community work around conversation, food, dance, healing, and Bali-Caribbean diaspora context.
- The strategic proposal materials repeatedly state that “the work is already real” and that people feel/change through it. This is a strategy-source claim, not independently verified client proof.

## Resistance

Theo explicitly resists fame-seeking visibility. He says he does not mind recognition for good work, but he does not want visibility to become performative or ego-driven. He also critiques commoditized spirituality and shallow social structures.

## Vision

Sourced future vision includes:

- a collective of healers
- retreat-style experiences
- corporate retreat/wellness pathways
- movable/agile delivery across locations
- possible playbook/framework for locations, resorts, and teams
- eventual larger healing/school/system vision that may include technology/frequency ideas

## What Must Be Protected

- The work should not be reduced to generic coaching.
- Visibility should not become fame-seeking performance.
- The collective/retreat vision should not cause Phase 1 overbuilding.
- Pricing, claims, and public copy require Major/human review.
